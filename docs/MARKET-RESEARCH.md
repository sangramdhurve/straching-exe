# StretchHome — Market Research

> Competitive landscape, demo-media benchmark, ASO, and positioning for a free,
> ad-supported, offline, all-ages, no-equipment, **stretching-only** Flutter app.
> Researched June 2026. Source URLs cited inline; full list at the end.

---

## 1. Top competitors

| App | Platform | Monetization | Demo media (the key question) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **StretchIt** | iOS + Android | Subscription (~$15/mo, free trial), no ads | **Real-human video classes.** Instructor-led, 100+ hrs of footage, multiple camera angles so you can see body position. Live classes too. This is the category's "premium" benchmark. [1][9] |
| **Bend** | iOS + Android | Freemium: free tier + Premium **$13.99/mo**; ad-light | **Custom 2D illustrations + countdown timer.** A static image of the correct position per move. No video. 10M+ users, NYT/Wirecutter featured. [2] |
| **Pliability (ex-ROMWOD)** | iOS + Android | Subscription **~$18–20/mo or ~$15/mo annual**, 7-day trial | **Real-human video**, 15–20 min guided sessions with narration + breathing cues; body-scan feature. CrossFit/mobility lean. [3] |
| **Stretching Exercises** (Leap Fitness) | Android (+Amazon), iOS | **Free with ads** + optional premium — closest model to StretchHome | **Looping animations** (animated figure) + some video, voice coaching. ~10M+ downloads. Mass-market freemium leader. [4][10] |
| **Sworkit / Stretch HD / Daily Yoga / FitOn** (senior lists) | iOS + Android | Mostly free / freemium | **Real-human trainer video** (Sworkit, Stretch HD's 60+ HD clips, FitOn celebrity-trainer classes). [6] |

**Takeaway on media:** the field splits cleanly into two camps —
**video (real human)** = StretchIt, Pliability, Sworkit, FitOn, Stretch HD; and
**illustration/animation** = Bend (still illustration), Leap Fitness (looping animation).
Nobody dominant in this consumer space leads with a **3D avatar** — 3D is mostly an
anatomy/strength-training niche (Muscle & Motion), not mainstream stretching. [1]

## 2. Demo-media benchmark

- **Category standard = real-person video loops** for the premium tier; **illustration/animation** for the free/lightweight tier. There is no third standard.
- **What users praise:**
  - Video: "having an instructor talk through each move kept me on track"; multiple
    camera angles make body position obvious. [1][7]
  - Illustration (Bend): some users *prefer* it — "much better than having a video
    instructor yell the same thing at you every day"; clean, calm, no fluff. [2][7]
- **What users complain about:**
  - "Some users **miss video tutorials** and find it inconvenient to rely on
    illustrations during exercises, which can **disrupt the flow**." [7]
  - The recurring unmet need is **"am I doing it right?"** — apps address it only
    indirectly (more camera angles, step text, safety cautions). Real form *feedback*
    requires live/AI pose detection, which almost none of these offer. [7]
- **Implication for StretchHome:** a static illustration alone is the weakest option for
  the "am I doing it right?" worry. A **short looping clip** (real human or a clean 3D
  loop) closes most of that gap without the cost of a full video-class library —
  exactly the middle ground StretchHome's current plan (GIF/MP4 loops) targets.

## 3. All-ages / 50+ angle

- A real, under-served segment: dedicated round-ups exist for "best stretching apps for
  **seniors**" (Mighty Health targets 50+, Freeletics has a 60+ track, Sworkit/Stretch
  HD pushed for older adults). [5][6]
- **What works for this audience:**
  - **Watch, don't read** — looping demo a user can mirror beats dense step text.
  - **Gentle-first framing** — "go only as far as is comfortable, none of it should
    hurt" language builds trust and reduces injury fear. [5]
  - **Seated / chair variants** are a recognized senior need (chairs are already in
    StretchHome's prop vocabulary — a real edge). [5]
  - **Large text, simple navigation, no jargon** — accessibility is the buying reason,
    not a nice-to-have. (StretchHome already ships large-text + high-contrast.)
  - **Trust signals**: "designed by professionals," clear safety notes, no pressure to
    subscribe. Free + no paywall is itself a trust signal for this group.
- Most premium incumbents (StretchIt, Pliability) skew young/athletic. **"Gentle,
  all-ages, no-equipment, free"** is a quadrant the leaders largely ignore.

## 4. ASO — keyword/phrase bank

Method: match what the app *does* tightly; mix 1–2 high-volume heads with longer-tail
phrases that are easier to rank. [8] Intuition tags below: **H** = high volume/competitive,
**M** = moderate, **L** = long-tail, lower volume, easier to win + higher intent.

**Title / subtitle candidates (head terms):**
1. stretching — **H**
2. flexibility — **H**
3. stretch app / daily stretch — **H/M**
4. mobility — **M**
5. stretching exercises — **M**

**Keyword field / long-tail (target these for ranking):**
6. stretching for beginners — **M**

## 5. Positioning gap for StretchHome

The incumbents leave a clear, defensible wedge:

- **StretchIt / Pliability** = premium, paywalled, athletic/young, needs internet for
  video streaming. Expensive ($14–20/mo).
- **Bend** = free-ish but illustration-only ("am I doing it right?" gap) and gated behind
  a $14/mo premium for most content.
- **Leap Fitness** = free + ads, broad — StretchHome's closest model — but generic
  animation and a crowded multi-app brand, not all-ages-trust-focused.

**StretchHome's differentiated wedge:**
> The only **free, 100% offline, all-ages, no-equipment, stretching-only** app with
> **looping demos** (not just static illustrations) and gentle/standard/deeper levels —
> built for the calm, trust-first, "watch-don't-read" user the premium athletic apps
> ignore.

Pillars to lean on: **Free forever (no paywall)** · **Works offline** (no streaming, no
account) · **All ages incl. 50+ & chair variants** · **Simple household props only** ·
**Stretching-only focus** (not buried inside a do-everything fitness app).

## 6. Actionable takeaways for UI & demo art-direction

1. **Move from static illustration → short looping demo.** The single biggest review
   complaint is "illustrations disrupt the flow / I can't tell if I'm doing it right."
   A 2–4s loop (real human OR a clean consistent 3D figure) per stretch is the
   highest-leverage upgrade. [7] This validates the planned GIF/MP4 direction.
2. **Use ONE consistent demonstrator in a neutral home setting.** Same figure, same
   plain background, same warm/calm palette across all 45 stretches. Consistency reads
   as "premium" far more than per-clip polish, and supports all-ages trust.
3. **Show 2 framings where position is ambiguous** (e.g. a front + side angle, or a
   "wrong vs right" callout). StretchIt's multi-angle approach is *why* users trust its
   form guidance — replicate the idea cheaply with a second still or alt loop. [1]
4. **Caption the demo with the cue, not the count.** Overlay a 3–5 word form cue
   ("keep back flat," "breathe out, ease in") synced to the loop — answers "am I doing
   it right?" inline without forcing users to read a step list mid-stretch.
5. **Make the gentle/senior path obvious in the art.** Default to a relaxed, neutral-age
   demonstrator and surface chair/seated variants visibly. Calm pacing + "go only as
   far as is comfortable" copy is the trust differentiator vs the athletic incumbents.
